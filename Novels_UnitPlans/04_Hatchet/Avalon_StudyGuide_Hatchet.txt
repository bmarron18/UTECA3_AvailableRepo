ChatGPT prompt--
	8th grade English ESL; "Hatchet" Gary Paulsen, Major Themes

1. Survival and Self-Reliance: The central theme of the novel is survival. Brian Robeson, the protagonist, must learn to fend for himself in the wilderness after a plane crash leaves him stranded. His journey is a testament to human resilience and the ability to adapt and survive under extreme circumstances.

2. Nature and the Wilderness: The novel highlights the beauty and danger of nature. Brian's relationship with the natural world evolves from fear to respect and understanding as he learns to live in harmony with his surroundings.

3. Isolation and Self-Discovery: Brian’s isolation forces him to confront his inner fears, insecurities, and thoughts. Through this, he discovers his inner strength and resourcefulness. The solitude helps him grow emotionally and psychologically.

4. Change and Transformation: Brian undergoes significant personal transformation throughout the novel. He changes from a dependent boy into a resourceful and resilient individual. This theme is mirrored in his physical transformation as well, as he becomes stronger and more adept at surviving in the wild.

5. Hope and Determination: Despite numerous challenges and moments of despair, Brian remains hopeful and determined to survive. His perseverance is a central theme, illustrating the importance of maintaining hope even in the most dire situations.

6. Technology vs. Nature: The novel contrasts modern technology with the primitive tools Brian must rely on to survive. His hatchet, a simple tool, becomes a symbol of his ingenuity and ability to make do with what he has in the natural world.

--- Chapter 1
In Chapter 1, Brian Robeson, a thirteen-year-old boy, is on a small plane flying from Hampton, New York, to the Canadian north woods to visit his father. Brian's parents are divorced, and he is struggling with the emotional impact of their separation. The pilot, a kind man, allows Brian to take control of the plane briefly, teaching him some basic maneuvers. Suddenly, the pilot suffers a heart attack and dies, leaving Brian alone in the cockpit. As the chapter ends, Brian realizes he must somehow land the plane safely by himself.

Vocabulary:
    Cockpit - the area of the aircraft where the pilot sits and controls the plane.
    Altimeter - an instrument that measures the altitude of the aircraft.
    Rudder - a primary control surface used to steer the plane.
    Heart attack - a medical emergency where the heart suddenly stops functioning properly.
    Divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage.
    Manoeuvers - movements or series of moves requiring skill and care.
    Compass - an instrument for determining direction.
    Throttle - a device controlling the flow of fuel or power to an engine.
    Terrain - a stretch of land, especially with regard to its physical features.
    Desperation - a state of despair, typically one that results in rash or extreme behavior.

Discussion Questions:
    How does Brian feel about his parents' divorce, and how is this affecting him as he begins his journey?
    What are Brian’s initial reactions when the pilot has a heart attack? How does he handle the situation?
    Why do you think the author chose to start the story with Brian learning to fly the plane? How does this foreshadow later events?
    Discuss the significance of the title "Hatchet." Why do you think the author chose this title, and what might the hatchet symbolize in the story?
    How does Brian’s experience in the cockpit with the pilot set the stage for his character development? What qualities does he demonstrate in this first chapter that might be important later in the story?
