8-Week Unit Plan
"Hatchet" by Gary Paulsen
####################################

	8th Grade English teacher; generate an 8-week unit plan for all chapters of Hatchet with Common Core Standards and daily activities

Here's an 8-week unit plan for Hatchet by Gary Paulsen, aligned with Common Core Standards and including daily activities.


Week 1: Introduction and Chapters 1-3
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.1: Cite textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.2: Determine a theme or central idea of a text and analyze its development.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Introduction to the book, author, and survival genre. Discuss the concept of survival and self-reliance.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 1 together. Vocabulary: drone, rudder. Discuss Brian’s situation and the setup of the story.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 2. Vocabulary: altitude, grimace. Group activity: Create a character chart for Brian.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 3. Vocabulary: slew, spasm. Discuss Brian’s first moments after the crash and his initial reactions.
    Day 5: Summary and reflection on Chapters 1-3. Create a timeline of key events so far.


Week 2: Chapters 4-6
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.3: Analyze how particular lines of dialogue or incidents in a story reveal aspects of a character.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 4. Vocabulary: abating, interval. Discuss Brian’s thoughts and survival strategies.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 5. Vocabulary: thrumming, consuming. Group activity: Discuss Brian’s physical and emotional state.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 6. Vocabulary: motivated, triggered. Write a diary entry from Brian’s perspective on his survival plan.
    Day 4: Discuss the themes of isolation and resilience. Create a theme chart.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 4-6. Group discussion on key events and Brian’s character development.


Week 3: Chapters 7-9
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.5: Analyze how a particular sentence, chapter, scene, or stanza fits into the overall structure of a text.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.6: Analyze how differences in the points of view of the characters and the audience create such effects as suspense or humor.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 7. Vocabulary: embedded, glacial. Discuss Brian’s physical challenges and problem-solving.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 8. Vocabulary: rasping, hummocks. Group activity: Draw and describe the setting of the wilderness.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 9. Vocabulary: kindling, tinder. Write a poem about Brian’s struggle to make fire.
    Day 4: Discuss the significance of fire and its symbolic meaning in the story.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 7-9. Group discussion on Brian’s growth and resilience.


Week 4: Chapters 10-12
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.7: Analyze the extent to which a filmed or live production of a story stays faithful to or departs from the text.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.9: Analyze how a modern work of fiction draws on themes, patterns of events, or character types from myths, traditional stories, or religious works.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 10. Vocabulary: dormant, exasperation. Discuss Brian’s changing mindset and strategies.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 11. Vocabulary: convulse, persistent. Group activity: Role-play a conversation between Brian and himself, reflecting on his growth.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 12. Vocabulary: shaft, gnarled. Write a letter from Brian to his parents about his survival experience.
    Day 4: Discuss the themes of hope and perseverance. Create a theme analysis chart.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 10-12. Group discussion on key events and themes.


Week 5: Chapters 13-15
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.1: Write arguments to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.3: Write narratives to develop real or imagined experiences.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 13. Vocabulary: corrosive, dormant. Discuss the changes in Brian’s character and survival skills.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 14. Vocabulary: unwittingly, incessant. Write a persuasive letter from Brian’s perspective about the importance of his survival skills.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 15. Vocabulary: shaft, unduly. Group activity: Create a storyboard of key events.
    Day 4: Discuss the theme of transformation. Write a short story inspired by Brian’s experiences.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 13-15. Group discussion on Brian’s growth and survival strategies.


Week 6: Chapters 16-18
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.8.1: Engage effectively in a range of collaborative discussions.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.8.4: Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 16. Vocabulary: vital, punky. Discuss Brian’s ongoing challenges and resourcefulness.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 17. Vocabulary: exulted, infuriating. Group activity: Design a poster representing Brian’s journey and survival techniques.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 18. Vocabulary: rivulets, oblivious. Write a journal entry from Brian’s perspective on his continued survival.
    Day 4: Discuss the themes of innovation and adaptability. Create a theme analysis chart.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 16-18. Group discussion on key events and Brian’s resourcefulness.


Week 7: Chapters 19-20
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.10: By the end of the year, read and comprehend literature at the high end of the grades 6-8 text complexity band independently and proficiently.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.2: Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 19. Vocabulary: substantial, oblivion. Discuss Brian’s final challenges and his reflections on his experience.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 20. Vocabulary: intact, futile. Write an informative essay on the themes of survival and self-discovery in the book.
    Day 3: Group activity: Create a timeline of Brian’s growth and changes throughout the story.
    Day 4: Discuss the overall impact of the survival experience on Brian. Write a reflective essay on Brian’s journey and what he has learned.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 19-20. Group discussion on key events and themes.


Week 8: Review and Final Projects
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.8.4: Present claims and findings, emphasizing salient points in a focused, coherent manner.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.7: Conduct short research projects to answer a question.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Review major themes, characters, and events. Group discussion on the entire book.
    Day 2: Begin final projects: Students can choose from a book report, a creative writing piece, or a multimedia presentation.
    Day 3: Continue working on final projects. Peer review sessions.
    Day 4: Finalize and present projects.
    Day 5: Unit reflection and celebration. Discuss what students have learned and how they can apply these lessons in their lives.
