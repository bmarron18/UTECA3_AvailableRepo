8-Week Unit Plan
"The Secret Garden"  by Frances Hodgson Burnett
####################################

	8th Grade English teacher; generate an 8-week unit plan for all chapters of The Secret Garden with Common Core Standards and daily activities

Here's an 8-week unit plan for The Secret Garden by Frances Hodgson Burnett, aligned with Common Core Standards and including daily activities.


Week 1: Introduction and Chapters 1-4
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.1: Cite textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.2: Determine a theme or central idea of a text and analyze its development.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Introduction to the book, author, and historical context. Discuss the concept of healing and transformation.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 1 together. Vocabulary: cholera, ayah. Discuss Mary’s life in India and her transition to England.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 2. Vocabulary: manor, moor. Group activity: Create a character chart for Mary.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 3. Vocabulary: sour, disagreeable. Discuss Mary’s first impressions of Misselthwaite Manor.
    Day 5: Read Chapter 4. Vocabulary: forlorn, bungalow. Write a diary entry from Mary’s perspective on her new life.


Week 2: Chapters 5-8
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.3: Analyze how particular lines of dialogue or incidents in a story reveal aspects of a character.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 5. Vocabulary: indignant, inquisitive. Discuss Mary’s curiosity about the garden.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 6. Vocabulary: scullery, typhoid. Group activity: Draw and describe Misselthwaite Manor.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 7. Vocabulary: alcove, contrary. Write a letter from Mary to her deceased parents.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 8. Vocabulary: perplexed, impudent. Discuss the significance of Mary’s discovery of the garden key.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 5-8. Create a timeline of key events.


Week 3: Chapters 9-12
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.5: Analyze how a particular sentence, chapter, scene, or stanza fits into the overall structure of a text.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.6: Analyze how differences in the points of view of the characters and the audience create such effects as suspense or humor.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 9. Vocabulary: tendril, grim. Discuss the symbolism of the secret garden.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 10. Vocabulary: wick, sprout. Group activity: Create a word web with descriptive words about the garden.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 11. Vocabulary: venture, coax. Write a poem about the garden’s transformation.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 12. Vocabulary: tend, crocus. Discuss the theme of friendship and Mary’s growing bond with Dickon.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 9-12. Group discussion on character development.


Week 4: Chapters 13-16
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.7: Analyze the extent to which a filmed or live production of a story stays faithful to or departs from the text.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.9: Analyze how a modern work of fiction draws on themes, patterns of events, or character types from myths, traditional stories, or religious works.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 13. Vocabulary: tantrum, fretful. Discuss Colin’s character and his impact on Mary.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 14. Vocabulary: invalid, fretful. Group activity: Role-play a conversation between Mary and Colin.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 15. Vocabulary: recluse, fret. Write a letter from Mary to Colin about the secret garden.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 16. Vocabulary: enchantment, dreary. Discuss the theme of healing and transformation.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 13-16. Group discussion on key events.


Week 5: Chapters 17-20
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.1: Write arguments to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.3: Write narratives to develop real or imagined experiences.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 17. Vocabulary: patron, affirm. Discuss the changes in Mary and Colin’s characters.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 18. Vocabulary: exultant, luminous. Write a persuasive letter about the importance of the garden.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 19. Vocabulary: vitality, rapture. Group activity: Create a storyboard of key events in the garden.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 20. Vocabulary: serenity, restoration. Write a short story inspired by the characters’ transformations.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 17-20. Group discussion on character growth.


Week 6: Chapters 21-23
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.8.1: Engage effectively in a range of collaborative discussions.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.8.4: Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 21. Vocabulary: revelation, serene. Discuss the theme of renewal and its significance in the story.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 22. Vocabulary: exuberant, vigor. Group activity: Design a poster representing the garden’s transformation.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 23. Vocabulary: rejoice, blossom. Write a journal entry from Mary’s perspective on the changes in her life.
    Day 4: Discuss the themes of growth and renewal. Create a theme analysis chart.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 21-23. Group discussion on key events.


Week 7: Chapters 24-26
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.10: By the end of the year, read and comprehend literature at the high end of the grades 6-8 text complexity band independently and proficiently.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.2: Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 24. Vocabulary: invigorate, revive. Discuss the final transformations of the characters.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 25. Vocabulary: serene, jubilant. Write an informative essay on the themes of healing and transformation.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 26. Vocabulary: reconciliation, harmony. Group activity: Create a timeline of the characters’ growth and changes.
    Day 4: Discuss the theme of reconciliation. Write a reflective essay on the overall impact of the garden on the characters.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 24-26. Group discussion on key events and themes.


Week 8: Review and Final Projects
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.8.4: Present claims and findings, emphasizing salient points in a focused, coherent manner.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.7: Conduct short research projects to answer a question.
Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Review major themes, characters, and events. Group discussion on the entire book.
    Day 2: Begin final projects: Students can choose from a book report, a creative writing piece, or a multimedia presentation.
    Day 3: Continue working on final projects. Peer review sessions.
    Day 4: Finalize and present projects.
    Day 5: Unit reflection and celebration. Discuss what students have learned and how they can apply these lessons in their lives.
