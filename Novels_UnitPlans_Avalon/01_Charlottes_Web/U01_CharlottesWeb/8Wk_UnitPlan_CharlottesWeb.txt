##################################
8-Week Unit Plan
"Charlotte's Web" by E.B. White
####################################
-- ChatGBT
	8th Grade English teacher; generate an 8-week unit plan for Chapter 1 through Chapter 22 of "Charlotte's Web" by E.B. White with weekly objectives, Common Core Standards and Daily Activities

Week 1: Introduction and Chapters 1-3
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.1: Cite textual evidence to support analysis of what the text says explicitly.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.2: Determine a theme or central idea of a text and analyze its development.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Introduction to the book, author, and historical context. Discuss farm life and key themes.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 1 together. Synopsis, Vocab and Discussion Questions
    Day 3: Read Chapter 2. Synopsis, Vocab and Discussion Questions. Group activity: Draw and describe the barn setting.
    Day 4: Read Chapter 3. Synopsis, Vocab and Discussion Questions 
    Day 5: Summary and reflection on Chapters 1-3. Create character charts for Wilbur and Fern.

Week 2: Chapters 4-6
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.3: Analyze how particular lines of dialogue or incidents in a story reveal aspects of a character.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.4: Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 4. Vocabulary: enchanted, salutations. Discussion on the first encounter with Charlotte.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 5. Vocabulary: middlings, gnaw. Write a diary entry from Wilbur’s perspective.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 6. Vocabulary: boasting, hullabaloo. Group activity: Draw a comic strip of Wilbur and Charlotte’s first meeting.
    Day 4: Discuss the relationship between Wilbur and Charlotte. Character analysis activity.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 4-6. Group discussion on key events.

Week 3: Chapters 7-9
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.5: Analyze how a particular sentence, chapter, scene, or stanza fits into the overall structure of a text.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.6: Analyze how differences in the points of view of the characters and the audience create such effects as suspense or humor.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 7. Vocabulary: conspiracy, injustice. Discussion on the importance of friendship.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 8. Vocabulary: sedentary, ascended. Group activity: Role-play a conversation between Wilbur and Charlotte.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 9. Vocabulary: gullible, versatile. Write a letter from Charlotte to Wilbur.
    Day 4: Discuss the themes of loyalty and friendship. Create a theme chart.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 7-9. Group discussion on character development.

Week 4: Chapters 10-12
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.7: Analyze the extent to which a filmed or live production of a story stays faithful to or departs from the text.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.9: Analyze how a modern work of fiction draws on themes, patterns of events, or character types from myths, traditional stories, or religious works.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 10. Vocabulary: radiant, radiant. Discussion on the power of words.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 11. Vocabulary: sedentary, merciless. Group activity: Create a word web with descriptive words about Wilbur.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 12. Vocabulary: spinnerets, phenomenon. Write a poem about friendship.
    Day 4: Discuss the theme of identity. Character analysis activity.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 10-12. Group discussion on key events.

Week 5: Chapters 13-15
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.1: Write arguments to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.3: Write narratives to develop real or imagined experiences.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 13. Vocabulary: idiosyncrasy, sedentary. Discussion on Wilbur’s growing confidence.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 14. Vocabulary: stowaway, phenomenon. Write a persuasive letter from Wilbur’s perspective.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 15. Vocabulary: anesthetic, phenomenon. Group activity: Create a storyboard of key events.
    Day 4: Discuss the theme of bravery. Write a short story inspired by Wilbur’s bravery.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 13-15. Group discussion on character growth.

Week 6: Chapters 16-18
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.8.1: Engage effectively in a range of collaborative discussions.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.8.4: Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 16. Vocabulary: miraculous, exertion. Discussion on the fair and its significance.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 17. Vocabulary: crisis, radiant. Group activity: Design a poster for the county fair.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 18. Vocabulary: exertion, miraculous. Write a journal entry from Wilbur’s perspective at the fair.
    Day 4: Discuss the themes of hope and perseverance. Create a theme analysis chart.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 16-18. Group discussion on key events.

Week 7: Chapters 19-22
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.8.10: By the end of the year, read and comprehend literature at the high end of the grades 6-8 text complexity band independently and proficiently.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.2: Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Read Chapter 19. Vocabulary: distinguished, phenomenon. Discussion on Charlotte’s plan for Wilbur.
    Day 2: Read Chapter 20. Vocabulary: solemn, distinguished. Write an informative essay on the importance of friendship.
    Day 3: Read Chapter 21. Vocabulary: triumphant, distinguished. Group activity: Create a timeline of Wilbur’s journey.
    Day 4: Discuss the theme of sacrifice. Write a reflective essay on Charlotte’s sacrifice.
    Day 5: Summarize and reflect on Chapters 19-21. Group discussion on key events.

Week 8: Review and Final Projects
Common Core Standards:
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.8.4: Present claims and findings, emphasizing salient points in a focused, coherent manner.
    CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.8.7: Conduct short research projects to answer a question.

Daily Activities:
    Day 1: Review major themes, characters, and events. Group discussion on the entire book.
    Day 2: Begin final projects: Students can choose from a book report, a creative writing piece, or a multimedia presentation.
    Day 3: Continue working on final projects. Peer review sessions.
    Day 4: Finalize and present projects.
    Day 5: Unit reflection and celebration. Discuss what students have learned and how they can apply these lessons in their lives.
